THEORY QUESTIONS

Q1: Why does a version scan (-sV) require root/sudo privileges?
A1: Version scanning requires root privileges because it uses raw packet techniques to probe services and read low-level network responses that standard user accounts are not permitted to access.

Q2: What is the risk of running a service with an outdated version on an open port?
A2: Running an outdated service version exposes the system to known vulnerabilities and exploits that have been publicly documented, allowing an attacker to gain unauthorized access or disrupt the service.
